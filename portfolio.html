Fake News Challenge: Cornell Data Science Fall 2018

  • In the Fake News Challenge, we sought an effective method to classify misinformation, as well as visualize properties of our methodology.
  • We split this task into stance detection and relevance detection. For our relevance detection, we implemented our own version of the C4.5 Decision Tree Algorithm and extended it to a Random Forest.
  • Our dataset comes from the FNC-1 website, consisting of article headlines, bodies, and stances.
  • Organization: Cornell Data Science - Insights Team
    •

Social Tribes: Cornell Data Science Spring 2018
  • Social Tribes was a project where we analyzed and visualized the effects of different Machine Learning clustering algorithms on Twitter data from political journalists.
  • The visualizations break the journalists into "clusters," grouped by people with the most follower similarity.
  • Organization: Cornell Data Science - Insights Team
    •
